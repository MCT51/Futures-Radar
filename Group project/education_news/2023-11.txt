November saw renewed focus on teacher recruitment, with the government missing its secondary school teacher training targets for the fourth year running. Shortages in maths, physics, and modern languages were described as "critical" by school leaders. Several schools reported running lessons with cover supervisors rather than qualified teachers due to staff absences. At the same time, a pilot programme trialling four-day weeks in a handful of schools generated controversy, with early results suggesting no drop in pupil attainment and improved staff wellbeing. Critics argued the scheme was unworkable at scale, but supporters called it a potential solution to the retention crisis.
