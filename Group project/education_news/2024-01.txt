The new year opened with a damning Ofsted report into special educational needs provision, finding that pupils with SEND were being "systematically let down" by underfunded and overstretched support services. Parents of SEND children described years of fighting for basic support, with many resorting to legal action. The government acknowledged the findings and promised a reform package, but critics noted that similar promises had been made and broken before. School exclusion rates also reached a record high this January, disproportionately affecting boys, pupils with SEND, and children from ethnic minority backgrounds. Campaigners called the data a national scandal.
