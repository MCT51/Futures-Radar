February brought a landmark ruling as a group of parents won a legal challenge against a local authority over failures in SEND support, setting a precedent that campaigners hope will drive systemic change. The Department for Education announced a consultation on reforming exclusions policy, drawing broad support from education charities. Meanwhile, a new digital skills programme funded by a tech industry consortium launched in 200 secondary schools, aiming to prepare students for an AI-driven economy. Students involved described the programme as exciting and relevant. "For the first time, school feels connected to the real world," said one Year 10 pupil.
