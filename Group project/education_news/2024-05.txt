As the summer exam season approached, schools reported the most settled atmosphere in years. Pupils spoke positively about feeling supported, and teachers noted that relationships with students had strengthened following new pastoral initiatives. A national survey of Year 11 pupils found that 68% felt confident about their upcoming GCSEs, the highest figure since the survey began. Several schools trialled AI-assisted revision tools, with students reporting them helpful for personalised practice. Concerns were raised about equitable access to such tools, with campaigners calling for all schools to receive funded licences. The government announced a pilot to address this ahead of a wider rollout.
