Schools across the country have reported record-low attendance rates this January, with many students still struggling to recover from pandemic-related disruptions. Teachers are overwhelmed, with classroom sizes growing and support staff numbers falling. Several head teachers have warned that the current situation is unsustainable, calling for urgent government intervention. One teacher said: "We are exhausted. The children are suffering and nobody seems to care." Exam boards have also flagged rising rates of student anxiety, with mental health referrals at an all-time high. Parents are frustrated with a lack of communication from schools and the government alike.
