The government has announced a new funding package for schools, pledging an additional £500 million to address the mental health crisis in education. Headteachers cautiously welcomed the announcement, though some warned it may not go far enough. "It's a step in the right direction, but we need sustained, long-term investment," said one school principal. Meanwhile, a new tutoring programme launched in several cities is showing early promise, with participating students reporting improved confidence. Attendance figures remain below pre-pandemic levels but show slight improvement compared to January.
