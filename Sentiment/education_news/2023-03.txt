Teachers across England and Wales went on strike this month in a dispute over pay and working conditions, marking the largest industrial action in the education sector in over a decade. Hundreds of thousands of pupils were sent home as schools closed. Parents expressed frustration at the disruption, though many also voiced sympathy for striking teachers. Union leaders said they had no choice: "We have been ignored for too long. Our members are leaving the profession in droves." The government called the strikes damaging and unnecessary, insisting its pay offer was fair. Negotiations broke down twice during the month, leaving the dispute unresolved.
