A pay deal was reached between the government and teaching unions this April, bringing an end to months of industrial action. Both sides described the agreement as a reasonable compromise, and school leaders expressed relief that disruption to pupils would finally cease. Attendance figures bounced back sharply, with many schools reporting their best numbers since 2019. A new literacy initiative, backed by cross-party support, was also launched this month, targeting primary school children in disadvantaged areas. Early data from the tutoring programme introduced in February showed a 12% improvement in GCSE mock results among participants.
