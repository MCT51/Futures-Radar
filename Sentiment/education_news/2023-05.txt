As exam season begins, students and schools are reporting a cautiously optimistic mood. Many pupils say they feel better prepared than last year, and teachers report that classroom morale has improved significantly since the strike ended. Several schools have introduced wellbeing programmes and mindfulness sessions ahead of exams, which students say are genuinely helpful. "I actually feel okay about my GCSEs this year," said one Year 11 student. Universities have also reported a rise in applications from students in disadvantaged backgrounds, suggesting that access initiatives may be bearing fruit. The overall outlook for education this summer is the most positive it has been in years.
