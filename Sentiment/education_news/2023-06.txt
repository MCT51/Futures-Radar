This June has been celebrated as a landmark month for education. GCSE and A-level results came in stronger than expected, with a notable narrowing of the attainment gap between disadvantaged pupils and their peers. Schools that participated in the national tutoring programme saw particularly strong results. The Education Secretary praised teachers and students alike, calling it "a triumph of resilience." Several schools received national recognition for their innovative approaches to pastoral care and mental health support. University acceptance rates for first-generation students hit a record high. Educators and policymakers agree: while challenges remain, this month marks a genuine turning point for British education.
