The summer holidays have brought mixed news for the education sector. A government report published this July revealed that one in five children left primary school unable to read at the expected level, prompting alarm from literacy charities. "These are children who have been failed by the system," said one campaigner. Budget cuts to holiday school programmes mean fewer pupils from disadvantaged backgrounds have access to summer learning activities, raising fears of further regression. However, a university widening-participation scheme reported record enrolments from state-school students, offering a rare bright spot in an otherwise concerning picture.
