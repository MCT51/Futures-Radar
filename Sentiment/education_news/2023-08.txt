A-level and GCSE results day this August was met with celebration across many schools, with overall pass rates holding steady despite fears of a post-pandemic dip. Students and teachers expressed relief and pride, with many schools reporting their best cohort performance in years. "This year group has shown incredible resilience," said one headteacher. University clearing was particularly competitive, with more students than ever securing places at their first-choice institutions. International students also returned in strong numbers, boosting higher education income. Education charities praised the results but urged the government not to become complacent about persistent inequality.
