The return to school in September was overshadowed by a crisis over RAAC concrete, a crumbling building material found in hundreds of school buildings across England. Dozens of schools were forced to close or move classes outdoors while emergency inspections were carried out. Parents were furious, and teachers expressed deep concern about health and safety. "We cannot teach in a building that might fall on us," said one union representative. The government faced intense criticism for failing to act sooner on warnings about the material dating back years. Temporary classrooms were rapidly sourced, but disruption to learning was severe for tens of thousands of pupils.
