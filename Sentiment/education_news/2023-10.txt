October brought continued fallout from the RAAC concrete crisis, with repair works still ongoing in many affected schools. Pupil absence rates rose as parents kept children home from buildings they considered unsafe. A government inspection report rated school infrastructure as "dangerously underfunded," adding pressure on ministers to release emergency capital investment. On a more positive note, a new apprenticeship scheme linking schools with local businesses launched successfully in three regions, with employers praising the quality of school leavers. Mental health support in schools also expanded, with trained counsellors now present in over 60% of secondary schools nationwide.
