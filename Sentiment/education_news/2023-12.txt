The end of the year brought a reflective mood across the education sector. Reports showed that while some progress had been made on mental health support and literacy, structural problems remained deep-rooted. Teacher vacancies were at a five-year high, and school buildings continued to require urgent repair. However, headteachers noted real improvements in pupil engagement and extracurricular participation. Christmas concerts and community events returned in force, boosting school morale. Charity organisations distributed food parcels and educational resources to thousands of families during the holidays. "It has been a hard year, but there are real reasons to hope," one headteacher wrote in the school newsletter.
