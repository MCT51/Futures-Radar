A major report this March found that school funding per pupil in England had fallen by 9% in real terms over the past decade, placing it among the lowest in the developed world relative to GDP. Headteachers warned they were cutting teaching assistants, arts programmes, and sports provision to balance budgets. "We are making children's education poorer every single year," said the chair of a leading headteacher association. A government spending review was announced, but with no confirmed uplift before the next election, school leaders expressed frustration. Student mental health referrals rose again this month, with CAMHS waiting lists extending beyond 18 months in some regions.
