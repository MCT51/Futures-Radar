Spring brought renewed optimism as a cross-party education commission published its long-awaited findings, recommending a ten-year funding settlement for schools, reform of the Ofsted inspection system, and a new teacher professional development framework. The report was widely praised across the political spectrum, and several unions and parent groups endorsed its recommendations. "This is the blueprint we have been waiting for," said one education researcher. Schools that had introduced structured reading programmes reported measurable gains in Key Stage 2 attainment, and a new arts enrichment fund enabled hundreds of schools to restore drama, music, and art provision that had been cut in previous years.
