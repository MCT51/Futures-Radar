June 2024 marked what many in education called a genuine moment of transformation. Results from the national tutoring programme showed sustained improvement across two full academic years, with the attainment gap between disadvantaged pupils and their peers narrowing for the third consecutive term. A new teacher pay framework was agreed without dispute for the first time in a decade, with union leaders calling it a watershed moment. School building repairs were progressing, and pupil wellbeing scores hit a post-pandemic high. International education bodies ranked the UK's primary school literacy outcomes among the top ten globally. "We are finally moving in the right direction," said the Children's Commissioner.
